# GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY — STACKSPEND

## 1. Exact target user

The primary user is a **Founding Engineer / Engineering Manager at 5–50 person SaaS startups** who is responsible for tool stack decisions but not formally in procurement.

Secondary users:
- Indie hackers running profitable side SaaS tools
- Early-stage CTOs managing fast-growing engineering teams
- DevTools-heavy startups (AI startups, agencies, YC-stage companies)

These users typically already pay for:
Cursor, GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT/Claude, and at least one API-based AI provider.

They rarely audit spend systematically — they only notice costs when invoices spike.

---

## 2. Trigger moment (what they search / feel)

They don’t search for “AI cost optimization tool.”

They experience friction first, then search things like:
- “why is my cursor bill so high”
- “how much does a startup spend on chatgpt per month”
- “copilot vs cursor cost comparison”
- “reduce SaaS subscription costs engineering team”
- “we are paying for both chatgpt and claude do we need both”

Or they hit a moment like:
- finance asks them to justify $500–$2000/month in AI tools
- they notice duplicate tools across teams
- onboarding new engineers increases seat costs unexpectedly

---

We would not rely on generic social posting.

Specific channels:

## 4. First 100 users (30 days, $0 budget)

### Week 1 — validation seeding
- Post “Show HN: I built a tool that finds wasted AI spend in your dev stack”
- Share in 3 devtool Discords with real screenshots
- DM 20 engineering managers from LinkedIn (small startups only)

Goal: 20–30 audits run

---

### Week 2 — community embedding
- Reply to Reddit threads about “AI tool cost” with actual insights + link
- Post breakdown: “What 10 startups are wasting on Cursor vs Copilot”
- Ask 5 founders to run audit and share feedback publicly

Goal: 50–70 total audits

---

### Week 3 — viral loop activation
- Optimize shareable audit URLs with OG images
- Encourage posting results: “I saved $240/month on AI tools”
- Feature leaderboard-style savings examples

Goal: first organic reposts on X

---

### Week 4 — compounding loop
- Collect case studies
- Repost “before/after stack optimization”
- Add lightweight “benchmark vs similar teams” messaging

Goal: 100+ audits, 10–15 shares

---

## 5. Unfair distribution advantage

Credex’s unfair advantage is **direct alignment with savings realization**.

Unlike typical SaaS tools, StackSpend is not just diagnostic — it directly connects to:
> “you are wasting money → here is how to reduce it immediately via credits or alternatives”

This creates a natural bridge:
Audit → visible savings → Credex consultation → monetization

Most competitors cannot monetize this insight loop because they do not control the credit supply side.

---

## 6. Week-1 traction definition

If successful, Week 1 looks like:
- 20–30 completed audits
- 5–10 shared results on social platforms
- 2–3 founders actively asking for deeper optimization help
- At least 1 high-savings case (> $500/month) engaging with Credex CTA
- First organic inbound traffic from Hacker News or Reddit

Even without virality, success is defined by:
> users voluntarily sharing their savings result without prompting

---

## Summary

This is not a mass-market SEO product.

It is a **high-intent, low-frequency, high-value audit tool** that spreads only when users discover real money loss in their stack.
